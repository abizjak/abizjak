MM Ch. II

Generally you can skip section 3 on sheaves and manifolds. While the examples described therein are interesting for a variety of reasons, they are not so relevant in the context of modelling logic.

Similarly section 7 on sheaves with algebraic structure is not so relevant for now.

In general if your topology is a bit rusty then the connections between different presentations of sheaves (sections 4-6) are going to be difficult to understand. If you have trouble understanding some detail do not worry. Later on the notion of a sheaf will be generalized and made more abstract, and the relevant properties proved at this higher level of abstraction. So do not spend too much time on these sections.

Nevertheless the more concrete presentations are useful for understanding the more abstract ones.

Exercises 3, 5, 8, 10, and 11 are not too relevant. The rest are good practice.
